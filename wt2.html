Work Term 2 - Fall 2025
With Scotiabank

Introduction

During my Fall 2025 co-op term, I had the opportunity to work as an Association Cyber Automation Analyst on the Cyber Integration & Automation team at Scotiabank. This page outlines the tasks I worked on, my goals, the skills I strengthened, and what I learned throughout this experience.

About Scotiabank

Scotiabank, also known as The Bank of Nova Scotia, was founded in 1832 and is one of Canada's largest financial institutions, serving over 25 million customers globally. They offer a broad range of financial services including personal banking, commercial banking, investing and wealth management. Their head office is located in Toronto, at 44 King street, but they have various offices across the world. The two offices I worked at during my term were the downtown head office and Scotiabank's Eglinton Campus in Scarborough.

44 King Street – Toronto Head Office

Eglinton Campus – Scarborough

My team's work was focused on developing solutions to support cybersecurity operations and improve processes across the bank. We used a Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response (SOAR) platform called Cortex XSOAR, developed by Palo Alto Networks. This platform is designed to help cybersecurity teams manage, automate and coordinate responses to cybersecurity incidents through automated workflows called playbooks.

Skills I Developed

In this role, I set out to deepen my technical skills, build my confidence communicating in a technical business environment and gain exposure to security tools and workflows. Some of the skills I developed and strengthened during this term were:

  • Python: This was the main programming language I used throughout my role, building automations within Cortex XSOAR. I used python to process large datasets, interact with REST APIs and generate structured reports using the Pandas library. I was first introduced to these tools for a data analysis project in a first year software design course and have developed my proficiency in them throughout various different courses at the University of Guelph, which allowed me to complete my work more effectively this term.
  • Documentation: I was tasked with creating confluence documentation for my work, so that it could be used by my teammates after my term was over. My coursework in group projects gave me a strong foundation in how to document my work, which I further developed in a professional setting. I also did some external reading online to understand the best way to structure my documentation.
  • Object-Oriented Programming: I applied OOP principles when developing automations in CORTEX XSOAR, using classes and instances to encapsulate functionality and improve code maintainability. My coursework at the University of Guelph introduced me to OOP concepts such as inheritance and encapsulation, and my work at Scotiabank reinforced my understanding of these concepts.

Major Project

My main project throughout the term was reporting on and synchronizing the UAT and Production environments of the SOAR platform used by my team. This project involved:

  • Developing Python automations using Pandas, difflib, and REST API calls to generate automated comparison reports between environments
  • Creating a detailed change management plan assessing impacts, testing procedures, rollout steps, and recovery plans
  • Presenting findings and recommendations to my team, including team leads and our director
  • Implementing the approved synchronization plan through automated XSOAR playbooks
  • Building automations that updated the team's jira with the environment synchronization status and metadata associated about the content in each environment
  • Creating Confluence documentation so my work could be reused and maintained by the team
  • Updating Jira regularly to communicate progress to the team

Learning Goals

At the beginning of the term, I outlined five learning goals to guide my development:

Learning Goal 1: Written Communication

  • Goal: To strengthen my ability to document code I write and technical processes in a clear and professional way for both technical and non-technical audiences.
  • Action Plan: I will write and maintain detailed documentation for SOAR playbooks and python automations I complete in my co-op job, through comments in the code itself and user guides on confluence. I will request feedback from my teammates when they review my code.
  • Measure of Success: Documentation is approved by my manager/teammates with minimal revisions required, and my documentation is effectively used without an additional explanation needed.
  • Reflection: During the work term, I significantly improved my ability to document technical work in a clear and professional manner. I was able to create detailed documentation for the python automations I developed and SOAR playbooks using both in-code comments and confluence pages, ensuring that others could use and build upon the work I had done at the company. Feedback from my teammates helped me refine the structure and clarity of the documentation, and by the end of the term my documentation required minimal revisions and was successfully used by the team without additional explanation needed.

Learning Goal 2: Cybersecurity Tools & Workflows

  • Goal: Build a deeper understanding of how a SIEM and SOAR interact in a large financial institution to strengthen cybersecurity defence.
  • Action Plan: Speak with my manager and team members about specific workflows and uses of the SIEM and SOAR platform at Scotiabank. I will observe how alerts are triaged and automated for personal learning.
  • Measure of Success: I will be able to clearly explain how a SIEM and the SOAR platform are integrated in the automated cybersecurity processes at Scotiabank.
  • Reflection: I developed a strong understanding of how a SIEM and SOAR platform interact within a financial institution. Through discussions with people in the organization, as well as hands on exposure to workflows in the SOAR platform, I was able to learn how alerts generated by the SIEM are ingested, triaged and responded to through the automated response platform. Working within real production and UAT environments allowed me to understand technical integration of these tools and the importance of automation in cybersecurity. By the end of the term, I was confident in explaining how these tools work together to support cyber incident response at Scotiabank.

Learning Goal 3: Organization & Time Management

  • Goal: Enhance my ability to manage priorities using Jira in a fast paced environment where I am assigned several tasks to work on.
  • Action Plan: I will use my team's Jira board to track my tasks, set timelines and keep others updated on my progress to ensure alignment with team priorities.
  • Measure of Success: Consistently meet task deadlines and receive feedback from my supervisor that I am reliable and organized.
  • Reflection: Working in a fast paced environment with multiple concurrent tasks required strong organization and time management skills. I used my team's Jira boards to track my assigned work, prioritize tasks and communicate my progress to the team. This approach allowed me to stay aligned with team expectations and adjust priorities when new tasks were introduced. Through this planning approach, I was able to meet deadlines reliably and manage long term projects alongside smaller requests.

Learning Goal 4: Problem Solving

  • Goal: Improve my ability to analyze cybersecurity challenges and apply creative automation solutions that strengthen incident response.
  • Action Plan: Work with my team to identify manual processes within the bank that are repetitive and can be automated and design playbooks to address them.
  • Measure of Success: Complete playbook automations that are successfully adopted within the bank, with positive feedback on their effectiveness.
  • Reflection: This work term strengthened my ability to analyze complex problems and design effective solutions. My main project during the term of synchronizing the team's production and UAT environments required me to break down the problem into manageable components and iterate through solutions. By doing this, I delivered automations that were implemented effectively and positively received by my team.

Learning Goal 5: Oral Communication

  • Goal: Develop stronger collaborative communication skills working within my team.
  • Action Plan: Participate in stand up meetings, sharing ideas and asking questions to learn more.
  • Measure of Success: Receive positive feedback during my mid-term evaluation about my ability to collaborate and contribute effectively to team discussions.
  • Reflection: Throughout the term, I actively participated in stand up meetings and team discussions, sharing my work and asking questions to deepen my understanding. I also had the opportunity to present a change management plan to my director, team leads and team members, which helped me build confidence in communicating technical work verbally. These experiences improved my ability to collaborate within the team and clearly explain my ideas in a professional setting. Feedback during the term indicated that I contributed effectively to discussions and worked well with others, meeting my communication goal.

Conclusion

This work term was both challenging and rewarding, and I am grateful for the opportunity to gain exposure to cybersecurity operations within a global financial institution. It allowed me to apply academic knowledge in a real-world setting and provided a new perspective on software development within cybersecurity.
